Who I am

Technologist From Togo 🌍.

I am a technology enthusiast from Togo, West Africa. I am working on my Computer Science Ph.D. at the Florida Institute of Technology (Florida Tech), where research deep neural networks and their representations for various predictive tasks. In my spare time, I hack on epic AI projects!

Here is how I normally operate:

Define

Before every project, I systematically define the objectives of the project. Well-defined project objectives allow for testable, verifiable, and valuable results.

Design

Next, I dive into the design, brainstorming architectures, structures, components of the project. Through quick analysis, I try eliminate as many potential issues as possible before building.

Build

With a satisfactory tentative design, I start building, one module or component at the time. That usually mean I workout the math, formulate algorithms, architect models, and write code. I iterate several times on the build, going back and forth between developing and testing, until I obtain a minimum viable product (MVP).

Launch

I deploy the MVP the quickest way I can for the world to see and critique. Based on user feedback, I iterate on the MVP until the product provides the intended value. Then the project enters its enhancement and maintenance phase.
