Who I am

The curious nerd from Lome.

My name is Josias K. Moukpe and I am a computer enthusiast from Lome, Togo. I am currently working on my PhD, researching in reinforcement learning. I completed my undergraduate from Florida Tech in Computer Engineering with a minor in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics. I recently co-founded Make-Print, a decentralized platform for people to connect and exchange additive manufacturing services such as 3D Printing, 3D modeling, Prototype post-processing.
Here is how I operate:

Define

Before every project, I systematically define the objectives of the project. Once the objectives of project are well defined, the project become verifiable and the product can be completed and tested.

Design

Next, I delve into the design, brainstorming the architecture, the structure, the components of the project, and through analysis, eliminate issues with the tentative design.

Build

Once satisfied with the design, I start building for the project, one module or component at the time. Depending on the project, I formulate algorithms, write software, design circuits, and build 3D models in CAD. Furthermore, I iterate several times on the project, going back and forth between developing and testing, until I obtain a satisfiable minimum viable product (MVP).

Launch

Once the MVP is complete, I deploy it for the world to see and critique. Based on user feedback, I iterate on the MVP until the product provides the intended value. The product is then launched and project enters its enhancement and maintenance phase.
