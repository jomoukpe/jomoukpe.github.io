Who I am

A Tech Fan from Togo.

My name is Josias K. Moukpe and I am a computer enthusiast from Togo.
Today, I am PhD student at the Florida Institute of Technology (Florida Tech), where research in algorithms, machine learning, and robotics. I completed my undergraduate at Florida Tech in Computer Engineering with minors in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics. In my spare time, I work on various project, including Make-Print, a Saas Startup to make 3D Printing easy and accessible to everyone.

Here is how I normally operate:

Define

Before every project, I systematically define the objectives of the project. Once the objectives of project are well defined, the project become verifiable and the product can be completed and tested.

Design

Next, I delve into the design, brainstorming the architecture, the structure, the components of the project, and through analysis, eliminate issues with the tentative design.

Build

Once satisfied with the design, I start building for the project, one module or component at the time. Depending on the project, I workout the math, formulate algorithms, write software, design models, etc. I iterate several times on the project, going back and forth between developing and testing, until I obtain a satisfiable minimum viable product (MVP).

Launch

Once an MVP is complete, I deploy it for the world to see and critique. Based on user feedback, I iterate on the MVP until the product provides the intended value. The product is then launched and project enters its enhancement and maintenance phase.
